SATD refers to comments where developers acknowledge that the current test implementation is incomplete, suboptimal, or relies on a compromise that should be addressed in the future. These admissions often appear as markers such as TODO or FIXME, or as notes about unresolved issues, temporary fixes, workarounds, hacks, performance limitations, use of deprecated APIs, unsupported features, poor design choices, skipped tests, or uncertain functionality.
