\section{Conclusion}
This paper presented a comprehensive study of method-level test code evolution. We found that existing history-generation tools generalize well to test methods without recalibration. For production-to-test mapping, no single approach consistently achieves both high precision and high recall. Using the most suitable techniques, we showed that test methods often evolve independently of their corresponding production methods and that highly evolving small test methods are more likely to contain test smells. We believe that our manually developed benchmarks, methodological advances, and empirical findings provide a more accurate foundation for future research on test evolution, regression testing, test obsolescence, and automated test repair.
